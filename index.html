About Celso

Embracing the unique benefits that being a multicultural technologist provides.

I have been a digital activist since 2009 when I was catalyzed into the undocumented youth movement because of my immigration status. Throughout my 6 years of digital organizing, I have learned digital skills that I noticed were usually missing in the immigrant community.

Through PochoLabs, my goal is to make these skills available to the immigrant community by offering various solutions for social justice organizations and immigrant-owned businesses.

This is a social enterprise, and as such, PochoLabs operates differently from other web development firms:

  1. Creating more Latino Tech Entrepreneurs: For big projects, instead of hiring or subcontracting web developers, I instead create a partnership with other Latino-owned firms or freelancers for that specific project. Instead of offering a job, I like to offer business opportunities to fellow Latino web developers.
  2. 50% of the projects are pro-bono: This is a bit radical, but necessary for both the immigrant community and for me to grow as a developer.
